Living Systems as Decentralized Economies

Why the Molecular Biologist Should Be an Entrepreneur, Not a Central Planner

BME 129C Capstone — Progress Report
Sage Clokey  |  Spring 2026  |  UC Santa Cruz

Goal & Approach

  • Central question: Are living systems literally decentralized economies — not metaphor, but measurable structural equivalence?
  • Thesis: Biological networks (PPI, GRN, metabolic) exhibit scale-free topology, distributed information flow, and robustness to failure — the same properties Austrian economists attribute to free markets
  • Method: Three layers of evidence from real data
    • Layer 1: Network topology (STRING, RegulonDB, KEGG) — no master node
    • Layer 2: Economic simulation — pathways as agents vs. central allocator
    • Layer 3: Cross-species trade — comparative advantage via codon distance

Accomplished So Far

Data Pipeline (Complete)

  • STRING PPI networks: E. coli (529 proteins, 6,951 interactions) and yeast (573 proteins, 6,342 interactions)

  • Centralized comparison networks (star, hub-spoke, lattice, random, BA model)
  • Economic simulation engine: distributed vs. centralized metabolic allocation

  • Automated figure generation pipeline
