## LinkedIn Post

The first job in the story of humanity was not king. Not priest. Not CEO. Not engineer.

It was gardener.

The man was placed in a garden and told to tend it. Not to design it. Not to optimize it. Not to scale it. To tend it. To steward something that was already growing.

Somewhere between then and now, we replaced the gardener with the engineer. We stopped asking "what wants to grow here?" and started asking "what can I build here?" We stopped tending systems and started controlling them.

The result is a world that builds faster than it understands, scales faster than it can sustain, and optimizes metrics that have nothing to do with life.

Every person is an agent. The question is what you serve. A Sagent — a sage agent — serves wisdom. Not a boss, not a market, not an institution. The pattern written into the structure of life itself.

The first job was gardener. The last job will be too.

From The Living Age — full chapter on Substack (link in comments).
