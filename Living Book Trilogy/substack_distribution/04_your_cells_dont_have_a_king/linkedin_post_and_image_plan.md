## LinkedIn Post

You have 37 trillion cells in your body. Every single one carries the same DNA — the same 3 billion base pairs, the same 23 pairs of chromosomes.

And yet a neuron looks nothing like a skin cell. A cardiomyocyte beating in your heart shares no visible resemblance to an osteocyte embedded in your femur. Same genome. Completely different expression.

No master cell told them what to become. No central command issued orders. Each cell read its own local environment — its neighbors, its signals, its position — and decided what to express.

The most sophisticated division of labor in the known universe is achieved without a king, without a CEO, without a central planner of any kind.

If you want to understand what a truly free civilization looks like, don't read political theory.

Read a biology textbook.

This is from my capstone research at UC Santa Cruz, where I mapped PPI networks, gene regulatory circuits, and single-cell transcriptomics to show that biological systems operate as decentralized economies — not top-down hierarchies.
