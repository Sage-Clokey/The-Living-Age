## LinkedIn Post

In Captain America: Civil War, the UN drafts the Sokovia Accords — a legal framework to put the Avengers under government oversight.

Steve Rogers refuses to sign.

"What if there's somewhere we need to go and they won't let us? What if there's somewhere we don't want to go and they make us?"

That's not arrogance. That's the recognition that centralized moral authority does not eliminate evil — it relocates it.

Consider the track record:
— Tuskegee: the government conducted the experiment
— Forced sterilization: the Supreme Court approved it
— MKUltra: the CIA authorized it
— Operation Paperclip: the US hired the Nazi scientists

Every case follows the same arc. The institution commits the atrocity, then positions itself as the necessary guardian against future atrocities.

The arsonist becomes the fire chief.

Captain America didn't reject accountability. He rejected the lie that accountability flows downward from institutions to individuals. True accountability flows upward — from each person to the moral law above them.

Every person is a moral node. Every conscience is sovereign.

The safest hands are still our own.
