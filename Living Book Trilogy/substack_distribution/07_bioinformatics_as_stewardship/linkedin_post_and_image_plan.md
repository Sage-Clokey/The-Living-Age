## LinkedIn Post

Every time you look for the master gene, you find a network.

Every time you try to identify the single controller — the one gene, the one protein, the one pathway that runs the show — you discover instead a web of interactions, feedback loops, and distributed decision-making that no single node controls.

This is the most consistent finding in modern biology. And almost nobody talks about what it means.

It means living systems are not machines. You cannot treat a genome like a blueprint, a cell like a factory, or an organism like a device to be repaired. They are not engineered. They are grown. And grown things have a grain — a direction they want to go, an internal logic no external authority wrote.

The machine model says: find the broken part and fix it. The garden model says: understand the conditions, tend the relationships, remove obstacles to growth, and observe what emerges.

The gardener does not make the plant grow. The gardener creates the conditions in which the plant can do what it already knows how to do.

Bioinformatics needs gardeners, not engineers. Stewards, not controllers.

The next era of biology will not be defined by who can control the most. It will be defined by who can listen the best.
