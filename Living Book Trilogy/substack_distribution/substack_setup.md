## About Page

Sage Clokey is the grandson of Art Clokey, who created Gumby in 1953 and spent sixty years refusing to sell. The son of Joe Clokey, who died holding the line. The third generation of a family that has been asking the same question for seventy years: how do you build something that survives the builder?

Sage graduated from UC Santa Cruz with a degree in biomolecular engineering and bioinformatics. His capstone research demonstrated that biological networks — protein interactions, gene regulation, metabolic pathways — operate as decentralized economies, not centrally planned hierarchies. The data didn't illustrate a philosophy. The data was the philosophy, written in molecular language two billion years before anyone used the word freedom.

The Living Age is his trilogy. Book I is the philosophy. Book II is the pattern in story. Book III is the biology that proves it. Three books. One seed.

He writes from the central coast of California, between the tech of San Francisco and the media of Los Angeles, in the place the Hobbits call the Shire.

## Why Subscribe?
