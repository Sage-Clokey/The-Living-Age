PHILOSOPHICAL NOTES (from Sage Clokey)

THE ANTI-PRUNING PRINCIPLE — Growth happens where it was meant to happen. Maintain what grows, don't cut it, help give it life. This is why Sage doesn't cut his hair. The same principle at every scale: don't prune what the Creator grew. The loom prunes timelines. The state prunes choices. The Darwinian model prunes the "unfit." The answer at every scale: steward it, don't cut it.

DIRECTED EVOLUTION — Evolution is not survival of the fittest where the unfit die. It is by choice of how information mixes. Mutations are not random — they are directed. Adaptation is more than what survives from random chance — it is directed information transfer at the molecular level. "Survival of the fittest" is the Sacred Timeline applied to biology: one path survives, everything else is pruned. The correct model: the Creator writes new code into the system in real time. Evolution advances through creation, not destruction. Natural selection is the editor, not the engine.
