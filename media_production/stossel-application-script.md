# Stossel Fellowship Application — 1-Minute Video Script

## Concept
Direct to camera. No cuts, no B-roll. Just Sage, standing with conviction. Outdoor setting — green lawn background.

---

## Script (approximately 55 seconds)

"My grandfather, Art Clokey, created Gumby — a small green figure made of clay. Clay doesn't command. It becomes. It transforms. Gumby was a quiet argument for freedom through creativity, not control.

I'm finishing what he started — through biology.

I'm a bioinformatics student at UC Santa Cruz. I've built a system that designs novel genomes from living organisms. The north star is living architecture — houses that grow, self-repair, and adapt. Not built from dead materials. Grown from living ones.

My grandfather showed the world what freedom looks like through clay and story. I want to show what freedom looks like through biology — that life organizes itself without central control, and we can design with that principle instead of against it.

You've spent your career showing people that freedom works. I can show them that nature has been proving it for four billion years.

Stories are seeds. I'm ready to plant one with you."

## Shooting Plan — April 9, 2026
- **Location**: Apartment complex lawn area, outdoor with green background
- **Time**: Morning — soft, even light
- **Wardrobe**: Button-up shirt, pants with belt
- **Stance**: Standing, framed waist-up
- **Camera**: Phone on tripod
- **Audio**: Lav mic clipped inside shirt placket near second button
- Face the light — sun behind the camera, not behind you
- Do a 10-second test clip and listen back with earbuds before full takes
- Eye line at the lens — practice the script enough to avoid glancing away
- Watch for wind — shield the mic or wait for calm moments

## Technical Notes
- Keep it under 60 seconds — aim for 55
- Speak at a natural pace, not rushed
- No background music
- Good audio is critical — Stossel's team cares about sound more than visuals
